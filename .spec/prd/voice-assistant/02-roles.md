# 02 - Roles

## Primary Personas

### 1. The Commuter (Primary)

**Profile**: Professional who commutes 30-60 minutes daily
**Context**: Driving, hands on wheel, eyes on road
**Goals**:
- Check calendar and email without looking at phone
- Send quick messages while driving
- Get news/weather updates during commute

**Pain Points**:
- Current assistants require screen glances for confirmation
- Siri/Google often misunderstand in car noise
- Can't correct mistakes without stopping

**Success Criteria**:
- Complete tasks 100% hands-free
- Understand speech with car noise/music
- Recover from errors via voice alone

---

### 2. The Hands-Busy Professional

**Profile**: Knowledge worker who multitasks
**Context**: Cooking, cleaning, exercising while wanting to stay productive
**Goals**:
- Capture ideas and todos without stopping activity
- Query information while hands are occupied
- Manage tasks during "dead time"

**Pain Points**:
- Can't type while cooking/exercising
- Loses train of thought waiting for responses
- Has to repeat context across sessions

**Success Criteria**:
- Quick capture of voice notes
- Natural conversation flow
- Context persists across interruptions

---

### 3. The Active Lifestyle User

**Profile**: Fitness enthusiast who exercises regularly
**Context**: Running, cycling, gym workouts
**Goals**:
- Control music and podcasts
- Log workouts and stats
- Check messages during rest periods

**Pain Points**:
- Sweaty hands can't use touchscreen
- AirPods touch controls are limited
- Phone in armband is inaccessible

**Success Criteria**:
- Voice control for all common actions
- Works with heavy breathing/movement
- Quick interactions don't break flow

---

### 4. The Accessibility-Focused User

**Profile**: User with visual or motor impairments
**Context**: Daily phone use with accessibility needs
**Goals**:
- Full app functionality via voice
- Clear audio feedback for all actions
- No reliance on visual confirmation

**Pain Points**:
- Many apps require visual interaction
- Screen readers are slow for quick tasks
- Error states are often visual-only

**Success Criteria**:
- All features accessible via voice
- Verbose mode for detailed feedback
- Clear error explanations

---

## User Segments by Priority

| Segment | Priority | Rationale |
|---------|----------|-----------|
| Commuter | P0 | Highest safety impact, clear use case |
| Hands-Busy | P0 | Large market, frequent use |
| Active Lifestyle | P1 | Growing market, tech-savvy |
| Accessibility | P1 | Important but smaller segment |

## Anti-Personas (Not Designing For)

- **The Skeptic**: Users who prefer typing/touch
- **The Privacy Absolutist**: Users who won't use cloud STT
- **The Power User**: Users needing complex multi-step macros
