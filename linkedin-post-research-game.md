# LinkedIn Post: Research Game in Agentic Engineering

**Hook Type**: Provocative + Question
**Post Type**: Thought Leadership
**Character Count**: 1,487 chars

---

Life moves pretty fast in agentic engineering. If you don't stop and look around once in a while, you could miss everything.

Ferris Bueller was talking about high school. I'm talking about the half-life of AI tooling knowledge.

The dirty secret of agentic engineering? Your AI is only as good as your research game.

Most engineers obsess over prompts, context windows, and model selection. They miss the real bottleneck: **information recency**.

That Claude agent you wrote yesterday? It's already working with stale patterns. The LangChain optimization you implemented last week? Superseded by a better approach that dropped three days ago.

This isn't about being bleeding-edge for clout. It's about **survival**.

Here's my system:

**Layer 1: Quick Lookups**
My `/research` skill hits a local Convex knowledge base first, falls back to web search if needed. Single-pass, 10-source synthesis. Takes 30 seconds.

**Layer 2: Deep Research**
When I need comprehensive coverage, `/deep-research` runs 5 iterative cycles with confidence scoring. Uses Jina's web/academic search + Exa's code context. Parallel fetching, cross-referencing, gap detection.

**Layer 3: On-Device Access**
I built a React Native app with Expo Router to query everything on the go. Convex backend with vector search means semantic lookups from my phone while I'm away from my desk.

**The MCP Integration**
Both skills connect via Model Context Protocol to my custom Holocron MCP server. FTS5 keyword search + vector embeddings. Sub-200ms hybrid search across my entire research corpus.

Is it overkill? Absolutely.

Was building a mobile app and custom MCP necessary? Not even close.

But here's the thing: flexing your builder muscles to stay informed isn't vanity. It's **compound interest** on your engineering capability.

Every pattern you capture, every research iteration you store, every API you integrate—it all feeds back into better agents, better tooling, better decisions.

**What's your research strategy for keeping your agents current?**
